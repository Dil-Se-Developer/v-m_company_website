Plan your visit

V&A South Kensington is now open seven days a week. General admission is free and you don't need to book.

Find out more about our return to opening seven days a week.

Please check the latest government guidance in advance of your visit.

  • We recommend that visitors wear face coverings, particularly within exhibition galleries and our more confined public spaces.
  • Our cloakrooms have not yet reopened. Bags larger than 56cm x 45cm x 21cm and bags with wheels are not allowed in the museum. Please note, only small sized bags are allowed in the galleries and as part of our security procedures we may ask to search your bag.

V&A Museum of Childhood will remain closed while it's being transformed into Young V&A – a new world-leading museum of design and creativity for children and young people. Find out more about the V&A Museum of Childhood transformation.

For self-guided formal education group visits, including schools, colleges and nurseries, please see our schools page.

STAYING SAFE

If you or anyone you live with has COVID-19 symptoms, or if you have recently been in contact with someone who has COVID-19, we ask that you please postpone your visit to the museum.

If you are a regular visitor you will notice signs and markers throughout the museum.

Hand sanitiser is available at the entrance and at key points around the gallerie.

We have installed protective screens at shop and café points and are regularly cleaning all areas of the museum throughout the day.

\ \

ACCESS

Entrance and exit

Please enter the museum on Exhibition Road and exit through the Members' and Tunnel exits. Exhibition Road is step-free and wheelchair accessible.

Our cloakrooms are currently closed. Bags larger than 56cm x 45cm x 21cm and bags with wheels are not allowed in the museum. Please note, only small sized bags are allowed in the galleries and as part of our security procedures we may ask to search your bag.

Timed entry for exhibitions is valid for 15 minutes – unfortunately late admittance will not be possible. Once inside, you are free to explore the museum for as long as you like.

If you'd like to reserve a wheelchair, email us at hello@vam.ac.uk 24 hours in advance of your visit.

Galleries

Over seven miles of inspiring galleries are waiting for you. Use our interactive map to explore, or pick up a paper map upon entry. We've also created a series of online trails to help guide your visit around the galleries. The Photography Centre, Rooms 99 – 101, is closed temporarily. The Tapestries galleries reopen in Spring 2023.

Exhibitions and displays

Booking for Fabergé: Romance to Revolution, Beatrix Potter: Drawn to Nature Fashioning Masculinities: the Art of Menswear and Africa Fashion is now open.

Find out about our new displays programme in What's On.

Please see our Membership page and reopening FAQS for more information.

FACILITIES

Toilets and cloakrooms

Our toilets are open throughout the building and are regularly cleaned throughout the day.

Our cloakrooms have not yet reopened. Bags larger than 56cm x 45cm x 21cm and bags with wheels are not allowed in the museum.

Please note, only small sized bags are allowed in the galleries and as part of our security procedures we may ask to search your bag.

Buggy park

A buggy park is available near the Exhibition Road entrance and Cromwell Road Entrance.

Find out aboutvisiting the V&A as a family.

Lifts

Lifts are operating throughout the building.

Shops

The V&A Main Shop, Fashion Shop and Exhibition Road Quarter Shop are open during museum opening hours. You may prefer to shop online. The V&A is a charity and every purchase made in our shops helps to support us.

Cafés

The Main Café is open from 10.00 – 17.00 Monday to Sunday, serving a range of hot and cold meals, cakes and pastries, hot and cold drinks, teas and coffee. The Garden Café is currently closed.

Garden

The John Madejski Garden is closed until 3 April 2022.
